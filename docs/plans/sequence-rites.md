# Sequence Rites

Status: baseline locked for worldbuilding. Sequence is now defined as a role-driven ritual relay rather than a dry civic abstraction.

## One-Sentence Definition
Sequence is the chained relay of witnessed rites that tells a place what state comes next.

## Core Rule
Time does not advance a settlement in the Hollow. Sequence does.

A district can remain in one state indefinitely if the next Turn is never completed. A place can feel like morning, crossing-time, homecoming, or sleepless aftermath not because hours have passed, but because enough people have performed the right relay.

People in the Hollow do not mainly ask, "What time is it?" They ask, "What Turn are we in?"

## Why Sequence Is Attractive
- it turns daily life into visible ritual drama
- it gives every settlement a pulse without using ordinary clocks
- it creates signature roles people can admire, fear, and obsess over
- it makes private relationships dangerous because they can interrupt or counterfeit a Turn

## The Five Turns

### 1. Facing
The settlement opens itself into public reality.

What happens:
- Bell Veils or local Facers sound the opening phrase
- residents answer under their held face, household sign, or sanctioned relation
- major crossings become singular enough to use safely

Character feel:
This is the most public Turn. It is elegant, exposed, and status-heavy.

### 2. Carrying
Witness is physically relayed through active routes.

What happens:
- Lantern Runners, pier crews, and route keepers carry sanctioned witness through crossings
- trade, errands, travel, and deliveries become stable enough to complete
- moving objects and people remain held between origin and destination

Character feel:
Fast, glamorous, dangerous. This is the Turn most likely to produce favorites.

### 3. Knotting
Rooms, promises, work, pairings, and local claims are tied into their current state.

What happens:
- doors receive knot seals
- tables, workshops, altars, and harbor lines are marked into current use
- household and pair relations are temporarily stabilized

Character feel:
This is the most intimate Turn. It is where legal and emotional meanings can diverge hardest.

### 4. Returning
People are called back to the relations that can safely keep them.

What happens:
- Return Singers, household callers, or kin-lines perform public and private recall
- routes begin favoring homeward relations over exploratory ones
- the lost, delayed, or thinned are given a chance to come back before drift worsens

Character feel:
This Turn is emotionally dangerous because "home" is never neutral.

### 5. Veiling
The settlement closes, seals, sleeps, mourns, and hides what must not remain exposed.

What happens:
- Last Lookers or paired witnesses seal sleepers, dead, shut doors, and latent routes
- shadows are tied, bedside witness is confirmed, and inactive spaces are made singular enough to rest
- unsafely unwitnessed people become vulnerable to drift, split states, or false summons

Character feel:
This Turn is beautiful, lonely, and full of forbidden private scenes.

## Exact Ritual Components
Sequence is created by a combination of five recurring elements:

1. bell phrase: the Turn is publicly called
2. answer-name: someone responds under a held face, role, or household sign
3. witness relay: light, gaze, or sanctioned presence is physically handed onward
4. knot mark: a room, door, body, or object is tied into a declared state
5. closing regard: another person or structure confirms the state before departure, rest, or concealment

If too many of these fail, a place may still appear normal, but it stops progressing correctly.

## Role Families Sequence Naturally Produces

### Bell Veils
Public openers of Facing. They are seen by everyone and allowed privacy by almost no one.

### Lantern Runners
Carriers of Carrying. Stylish, exposed, and physically trusted more than most people can survive.

### Knotkeepers
Specialists of Knotting who bind spaces, agreements, and temporary pair-states into place.

### Return Singers
People whose voices, names, or household calls can pull others back into safe relation.

### Last Lookers
Witnesses of Veiling who confirm sleep, endings, and closure. They should feel beautiful and slightly unbearable.

## What Makes Sequence Dangerous
- a false call can push a district into the wrong Turn
- a wrong answer-name can make a route or room hold the wrong person true
- a broken relay can cause misarrival mid-Carrying
- an illegal knot can preserve a forbidden bond past the point it should hold
- a failed Return can make a person easier for another household, rival witness, or hostile place to claim
- an unwitnessed Veiling can turn sleep into drift exposure

## Sequence Failure States

### Stalled Facing
The settlement never cleanly opens. Public routes remain uncertain and status becomes physically unstable.

### Broken Carrying
People and goods move, but not reliably to the intended relation. This produces stylish and terrifying misdeliveries.

### False Knotting
A room, vow, or pair-state is Held True under the wrong relation. This is a major source of forbidden intimacy.

### Missed Return
Someone who should have been called home remains relationally available to other claims.

### Unveiled Night
Sleepers, dead, and quiet spaces do not seal correctly. This makes the settlement feel dangerously unfinished.

## Common Vigil Relationship
The Common Vigil manages public Sequence, especially Facing, Carrying, and large-scale Returning.

But households, pairs, found families, dock crews, shrine circles, and illegal witness networks all create local Sequence pressure too. This keeps the world personal.

## First Major Character Conflict Turn
Selected Turn: Returning.

### Why Returning Wins
- it asks the most character-loaded question in the Hollow: who gets to call you home?
- it lets Common Vigil order collide directly with private Keeping
- it makes voice, belonging, dependence, and refusal mechanically and emotionally inseparable
- it naturally creates favorite-character roles such as Return Singer, Backcaller, and the person caught between them

### Core Conflict Image
Two voices call the same person across the Vigil Span: one public, one private. Only one call is lawful. Both may be sincere.

## Character Appeal Rule
Sequence should not feel like a schedule. It should feel like a chain of scenes and roles people want to see:

- the Bell Veil beginning Facing with a cracked halo
- the Lantern Runner carrying witness across a route that wants someone else
- the Knotkeeper tying two people into the same room under a relation they should not share
- the Return Singer whose call someone wants to obey and wants to resist
- the Last Looker who is the final person allowed to see you before sleep or disappearance

## Prototype Relevance
The Vigil Span should mostly teach Facing and Carrying first, with hints of failed Returning and dangerous Veiling.

That gives the first area momentum, style variety, and character-role clarity.

## Design Rule
If Sequence can be summarized as "their version of timekeeping," the concept has failed.
