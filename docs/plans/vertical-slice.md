# Vertical Slice Plan

Status: active again. The world law is now stable enough to restart slice planning.

Current baseline decisions are now defined more concretely in `control-and-slice.md`, `prototype-tuning.md`, and `yun-first-wrong-home-setpiece.md`.

## Slice Thesis
The first playable must prove that the Hollow is a game of relation, risk, and skill rather than a story wrapper with unusual lore.

## Hard Requirement
If the slice can be converted into a visual novel without losing its identity, the slice is failing.

## Slice Goals
The first playable must prove all of these:

- the world is visibly void and non-terrestrial
- physics is visibly independent of time
- one relationship or institution can physically distort reality through witness and relation
- the player must actively read, maintain, and exploit route truth in motion
- at least one mechanically wrong arrival must create forward consequence rather than simple failure

## Player Verbs To Prove
- read witness truth
- qualify for passage
- maintain a crossing under pressure
- deliberately exploit Held Wrong or misarrival
- carry the social consequence of how a route was crossed

## Target Prototype Length
- 20 to 30 minutes for a straightforward clear
- 45 to 60 minutes including optional mastery and wrong-route discovery

## Core Slice Structure

### 1. Sanctioned Baseline
Teach one stable Sequence Road and one readable Witness Bridge so the player learns what correct public infrastructure feels like.

### 2. Thinning Recovery
Force the player to restore a weakening route before full failure lands.

### 3. Portable Witness Labor
Introduce one carry action or witness source that makes movement feel like active maintenance instead of passive walking.

Current baseline:
Relay Lantern.

### 4. Held Wrong Temptation
Present a coherent but incorrect route that looks useful enough to take on purpose.

Current reward baseline:
Frayed Bell Thread plus social suspicion.

### 5. Wrong Arrival Consequence
Let the player continue from a misarrival or false Returning outcome rather than cutting to a simple retry.

### 6. Changed Revisit
Bring the player back through a space that now recognizes them differently.

## Success Criteria
- A player can explain the difference between Thinning, Held Wrong, and Lost without opening a glossary.
- At least one route is easier but socially or emotionally worse.
- At least one failure result advances the state of the area instead of only reloading a checkpoint.
- At least one character or institution reacts differently based on how the player arrived.
- Non-interactive exposition should not dominate the slice.
- The slice should produce one obvious mastery challenge for replay.
- The slice should feel mechanically fun within the first 30 seconds of active crossing, not only after the player understands the lore.

## Current Recommended Setup
- Perspective anchor: Lune.
- Baseline zone: the Vigil Span.
- Safe baseline: Common Vigil infrastructure.
- Dangerous alternative: a private or hostile witness route that can be used deliberately.
- Contrast case: one light Name Door or Weight Current.
- Control model: fully real-time, 2D oblique action view, with focus-hold route reading.
