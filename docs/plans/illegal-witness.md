# Illegal Witness

Status: baseline locked for worldbuilding. This is the Hollow's main source of forbidden intimacy, secret routes, and dangerous pair-bonding.

## Public Term
Counterwitness.

## Street Terms
- Keeping
- private hold
- backcall
- wrong home

## One-Sentence Definition
Counterwitness, usually called Keeping, is the illegal act of privately holding a person, room, or route true outside Common Vigil sanction.

## Why It Exists
Because people do not accept public reality quietly.

They keep lovers who were Turned Away.
They keep siblings who should have drifted.
They keep the nearly dead from becoming Unkept.
They keep a room belonging to the wrong pair.
They keep one voice as home even when the Common Vigil says that home is false.

The Hollow makes private attachment physically potent. Keeping is what happens when someone decides that power is worth the ruin.

## Why It Is Addictive
- to be kept is to be chosen over public truth
- to keep someone is to become the place they return to
- it creates secret crossings, wrong homes, and pair-only states the world should not allow
- it turns affection, obsession, guilt, and grief into something physically undeniable

## The Core Rule
Public witness keeps society usable.
Private Keeping makes reality personal.

It is usually stronger, hotter, and more unstable than sanctioned witness because it is concentrated through fewer people and more dangerous feeling.

## Main Forms Of Keeping

### 1. Backcall
An illegal Returning performed by one person or one private circle.

Effect:
Someone begins treating the backcaller's presence as home, even if public routes disagree.

Appeal:
Possessive, romantic, desperate, and immediately character-driven.

### 2. Pair Knot
Two people or a tiny group are bound into a room, promise, bedspace, hideout, or route state that should not legally hold.

Effect:
A place stays true for the pair under a private relation instead of the public one.

Appeal:
This is the Hollow's most direct "us against the world" ritual form.

### 3. Shadow Share
Two people exchange or stitch parts of their shadows so they remain near across blind wards or route loss.

Effect:
Distance collapses unnaturally between them, but so does clean individuality.

Appeal:
Stylish, intimate, and dangerous to animate.

### 4. False Halo
Stolen, copied, or damaged witness gear is used to mimic sanctioned route truth.

Effect:
Routes hold wrong rather than true. This allows access, pursuit, escape, and seduction under false legitimacy.

Appeal:
The most glamorous and visibly criminal form of Keeping.

### 5. Afterkeep
Someone continues holding a person who should already be dead, gone, or publicly unrecoverable.

Effect:
The kept person may remain present, speakable, reachable, or lovable, but no longer in a stable, lawful way.

Appeal:
This is where grief, romance, denial, and horror fuse hardest.

## Costs Of Keeping

### Overhold
The kept person becomes too specifically real to one witness and less real everywhere else.

### Misbond
Someone begins answering to the wrong home, wrong partner, wrong room, or wrong name.

### Pair Drift
If one side thins, the other begins destabilizing with them.

### Public Fading
The Common Vigil may reduce a person's standing until public routes stop holding them safely.

### False Return
A kept person can come back beautifully and still come back wrong.

## What The Common Vigil Thinks
The Common Vigil calls Keeping dangerous because it privatizes the power to decide what is real.

This is not propaganda. It is also true.

But the Vigil cannot erase why people still do it:

- public witness is often colder than love
- lawful reality can be unbearable
- some people would rather be kept wrongly than lost correctly

## Role Types Keeping Produces

### Backcaller
Someone whose voice can illegally become another person's home.

### Shadow Stitcher
A person who handles shadow exchange, route stitching, and pair-drifts.

### Halo Thief
Someone who steals or counterfeits witness halos and route legitimacy.

### Afterkeeper
A person holding someone past the point the world thinks they should still be reachable.

### The Kept
The person being held outside sanction. They are never just passive; their dependence and consent matter.

## Visual Language
- stolen bell halos worn cracked or off-center
- paired shadow seams on skin, clothing, or route marks
- lanterns that burn with too-personal colors or wrong directional pull
- knot cords tied beneath sleeves, collars, or bed frames
- mirrored sigils that only make sense when two people stand close enough

The broader fashion and strata split for Keeping is defined in `keeping-subculture.md`.

## Why It Fits The Hollow
Keeping lets the world law become emotionally unfair in the right way.

It turns private attachment into forbidden infrastructure.
It makes pairings physically meaningful.
It gives the setting a source of glamour, secrecy, and emotional excess without reducing the world to pure social theory.

## First Major Conflict Tie-In
Keeping is most dangerous during Returning.

When the world asks who gets to call you home, public witness and private desire become impossible to separate.

## Design Rule
If Keeping ever reads as a generic rebel faction, a simple black market, or just a cooler alternative to the Common Vigil, it has failed. It must remain intimate, alluring, and ruinously specific.
