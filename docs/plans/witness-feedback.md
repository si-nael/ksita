# Witness Feedback

Status: baseline locked for prototype readability. The exact assets can change, but the communication model should remain stable.

## Core Decision
Witness state should be communicated diegetically first, with a subtle systemic fallback.

The player should usually understand route truth from the world, the body, and sound before any explicit UI explains it. However, the prototype should still be readable enough that failure feels deserved rather than cryptic.

## Witness Model
Witness is both a local traversal state and a broader narrative standing.

### Local State
Moment-to-moment route truth.

This is what the player reads through Held True, Thinning, Held Wrong, and Lost.

### Standing
Longer-term social and relational recognition.

This affects:
- who may use certain routes
- who gets believed or restored
- how institutions and households hold the character
- whether the world treats someone as publicly safe, suspect, fading, or nearly unreal

## Player-Facing Rule
Do not turn Standing into a permanent universal meter.

Local state should be read immediately.
Standing should be felt through access, dialogue, ritual response, route permissions, and visible marks.

## Readability Goal
Within about one second, the player should be able to tell which of these is true:

- the route is holding correctly
- the route is weakening
- the route is holding incorrectly and will misdeliver
- the route has stopped recognizing the traveler at all

## The Three Feedback Layers

### 1. World Layer
The space itself states whether the traveler is being correctly witnessed.

Primary signals:
- bell rhythm and harmony
- lantern gaze direction and flame posture
- bridge filament tension
- inscription stability
- watcher statues, tower eyes, or window apertures turning toward or away from the traveler

### 2. Body Layer
The traveler's body should betray route truth even before full failure.

Primary signals:
- shadow attachment or shadow lag
- silhouette coherence
- footstep placement certainty
- afterimage splitting
- breath or voice echo alignment

### 3. System Layer
The game may use a minimal non-diegetic reinforcement layer, but never a dominant resource bar.

Allowed forms:
- subtle screen-edge coherence pulse
- slight vignette change keyed to witness state
- small proximity sigil near the traveler in prototype clarity mode

Forbidden form:
- permanent HUD meter that turns witness into a generic bar-management mechanic

## The Four Witness States

### 1. Held True
The route is witnessing the traveler correctly.

World:
- paired bells ring in the same phrase
- lantern flames lean forward along the route
- bridge strands look taut and singular

Body:
- footsteps land exactly where expected
- shadow remains attached and centered
- no afterimage separation

Audio:
- clear single bell tone
- one stable footstep echo

Meaning:
The player can cross safely if they maintain the route condition.

### 2. Thinning
Correct witness is weakening but has not yet failed.

World:
- one bell lags half a beat behind the other
- lantern gaze flickers or hesitates
- route edges fray or double briefly

Body:
- shadow trails by a fraction of a step
- silhouette shimmers at the outline
- breath or cloth motion desyncs slightly from movement

Audio:
- mild phase wobble
- faint doubled resonance on impact sounds

Meaning:
The player still has a grace window, but should correct the relation immediately.

### 3. Held Wrong
The route is active, but it is witnessing the traveler under the wrong relation and will misdeliver.

World:
- bells resolve into a stable but wrong harmonic answer
- lanterns point toward a path that is visually valid yet relationally false
- bridge geometry looks intact but subtly mirrored, offset, or too symmetrical

Body:
- the shadow pulls sideways rather than backward
- a second footstep echo arrives from the wrong destination
- the traveler's outline remains stable but feels slightly displaced from the route center

Audio:
- stable sound, but in the wrong register or from the wrong spatial direction
- whispered naming that does not match the current intended relation

Meaning:
This is not simple danger. It is a false success. If the player continues, they arrive somewhere real but unintended.

### 4. Lost
No valid witness is currently holding the traveler.

World:
- bells lose directional meaning or fall silent
- lanterns refuse to face the traveler
- the route shows too many possible positions at once or none at all

Body:
- shadow detaches or vanishes
- outline frays into multiple candidate poses
- footsteps stop sounding anchored to the surface

Audio:
- impact sounds lose location
- ambient void pressure replaces route resonance

Meaning:
Drift, split passage, or route collapse is imminent.

## Hard Readability Rules
- False witness must never look identical to thinning witness.
- Every failure state must be telegraphed before the player is punished.
- The body layer must confirm what the world layer implies.
- No critical state should be communicated by color alone.
- Audio must carry equal weight with visuals so the system remains readable in motion.

## Prototype Teaching Order

### Step 1
Teach Held True in a safe space with exaggerated paired-bell feedback.

### Step 2
Teach Thinning through a recoverable route where the player can restore witness quickly.

### Step 3
Teach Lost through a short, low-punishment drift event.

### Step 4
Teach Held Wrong only after the player already trusts the witness language.

Held Wrong should feel upsetting because the player understands the route looked stable for the wrong reason.

## Vigil Span Application

### Bell-Eyes
Each major bridge is governed by two bell-eyes. When both recognize the traveler under the same relation, the bridge is Held True.

### Lantern Witness
Portable or mounted witness lanterns can extend correct route authority through blind sections.

### Blind Wards
Areas with no valid witness should not simply be dark. They should feel unreadable, as if the route is refusing singular shape.

### Hostile Witness
Damaged towers, false saints, or predatory observer-things can hold a route incorrectly. Their feedback must look coherent enough to tempt the player, but off enough to read if the player is attentive.

### Civic Context
Most readable, stable witness in the prototype should come from Common Vigil infrastructure. This gives players a baseline for what sanctioned witness feels like before the game introduces damaged, hostile, or illegal alternatives.

## Prototype UI Policy
Default target: diegetic-first, no permanent witness meter.

Prototype allowance:
- optional clarity mode may add a small state sigil near the player
- early tutorial segments may briefly label the four witness states directly

The long-term goal is not opacity. It is confidence without generic UI dependence.

## Design Rule
If a player fails witness travel and cannot say whether they were ignored, misrecognized, or thinning, the feedback system is not ready.
