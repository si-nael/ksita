# Subculture Direction

## Purpose
This project should feel like a world people want to obsess over, not just respect from a distance.

The Hollow should not read like dusty myth notes, classical literature homework, or a social-systems essay with fantasy paint. It should feel sharp, addictive, mysterious, emotional, and character-driven in the way strong light-novel or anime-original worldbuilding often does.

## Target Feel
- a void fantasy with stylish silhouettes and dangerous intimacy
- a world that looks cool before it is explained and hurts more after it is understood
- a setting full of roles, titles, relics, and forbidden pairings people want to dig into
- enough mystery to fuel theorycrafting without becoming unreadable
- visually 2D-first and designed to look iconic in still frames, not realistic in motion

## Core Appeal Pillars

### 1. Hook First, Thesis Later
Any world rule should first land as an image, person, title, or scene that makes the audience lean in.

### 2. Every System Needs A Face
Sequence, witness, drift, route failure, exile, and disanchoring should never stay abstract for long. Each needs a role, outfit, relic, or person attached to it.

### 3. Poster Energy Matters
Major groups and roles need instantly recognizable silhouettes, props, and naming.

### 4. Private Rituals Beat Public Explanation
The most memorable worldbuilding should come from intimate practices between two or three people, not from detached exposition.

### 5. The World Must Produce Favorites
The setting is not finished if it only creates admiration. It should create bias, fixation, favorite pairings, favorite factions, and character arguments.

### 6. Mystery Should Invite Attachment
The audience should want to know more because the unknown belongs to someone compelling, not because the lore document is incomplete.

## Language Direction
- Use clear, sharp, image-heavy language.
- Prefer striking names over abstract categories.
- Avoid archaic diction, pseudo-biblical grandeur, and lecture-like explanation.
- Prefer terms that could work as episode titles, faction names, or character roles.

## Visual Tone Rule
If a visual direction would look more generic after becoming more realistic or more dimensional, reject it.

## World-Specific Appeal Targets
- bell halos that align over the head when witness locks in
- face veils with shifting insignia
- lantern harnesses that make the carrier look both sacred and hunted
- detached shadows that behave like second selves
- route scars, black sea reflections, split silhouettes, and upside-down crossings

## Design Conversion Rule
When a concept feels too institutional or essay-like, convert it using at least two of these:

1. give it a signature role
2. give it a signature relic or prop
3. give it a private ritual
4. give it a pair or rivalry dynamic
5. give it a visual state change the player can recognize instantly

## Tone Check
If a paragraph makes the world sound respectable but not desirable, rewrite it.

If a concept sounds smart but does not make you want to see the people inside it, rewrite it.
