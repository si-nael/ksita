# Gameplay Identity

## Purpose
Prevent the Hollow from collapsing into a visual novel, walking simulator, or story wrapper with light interaction.

## Core Demand
The player must repeatedly do things that only this world law allows.

Story is important, but it cannot be the main vehicle carrying the project's identity. The project's identity should come from active traversal, witness control, route risk, misarrival, social consequence, and mastery.

## Innovation Thesis
The game's innovation should not come from a one-time plot twist or a strange setting summary.

It should come from the fact that recognition, relation, and emotional attachment are the actual geometry of play.

## Required Gameplay Pillars

### 1. Read Route Truth
The player must read the world in motion.

This includes:
- identifying which route authority matters
- distinguishing Held True, Thinning, Held Wrong, and Lost
- predicting danger before punishment lands

If the player can ignore route signals and still succeed, the system is too shallow.

### 2. Qualify For Passage
The player must change or carry the right relation to move.

This includes:
- carrying a witness source
- using the right name, role, or claim
- accepting or shedding burden
- entering under public or private recognition

If a path opens because the player merely pressed a switch, it is not Hollow traversal.

### 3. Maintain A Crossing Under Pressure
Movement should be performance, not autopilot.

This includes:
- staying inside line-of-witness
- preserving bell cadence or lantern alignment
- crossing while a route is thinning
- keeping one truth alive while another force tries to rewrite it

The player should feel that crossing a route is labor.

### 4. Use False Success Deliberately
Held Wrong and misarrival should be playable, not just punitive.

This includes:
- intentionally taking a wrong-but-useful route
- accepting bad arrival to reach forbidden space
- deciding whether survival, speed, secrecy, or correctness matters more

If failure only means death or reset, the Hollow is not using its own strengths.

### 5. Carry Social Consequence Forward
How the player crossed should matter after the crossing ends.

This includes:
- changed access to people or institutions
- suspicion, trust, or altered standing
- revisits that remember the player's previous route behavior
- relationships worsening because the player arrived wrongly, late, or under the wrong witness

If traversal and story are separable, the design is too conventional.

### 6. Reward Mastery And Reinterpretation
The player should gain more than raw completion by learning the world's strange logic.

This includes:
- optional mastery routes
- alternate arrival states
- challenge crossings with tighter readability demands
- replay routes that reveal different truths because the player now understands how to break or hold relation on purpose

If second-play understanding does not meaningfully deepen play, the project is leaving value on the table.

## Moment-To-Moment Verbs
- read
- qualify
- carry
- hold
- break
- misarrive
- return

These verbs should do more identity work than dialogue choices.

## Chapter Rule
Every chapter should contain at least one player-owned contradiction that cannot be resolved through dialogue alone.

Good examples:
- crossing under a witness that is becoming false
- deciding whether to use sanctioned or illegal recognition
- choosing who gets returned correctly and who gets held wrongly
- revisiting a place whose relation changed because of an earlier choice

Bad examples:
- long exposition followed by a token traversal scene
- dialogue branches that decide outcomes without mechanical risk
- puzzle doors that could exist in any fantasy game with different art

## Anti-Patterns
- cutscene-first chapter structures where play only fills the gaps between story beats
- generic stealth with eye-shaped enemies but no relation logic
- platforming that still works if witness rules are removed
- route puzzles that are really just color-code locks with Hollow terminology on top
- optional content that adds hours by adding text instead of new play

## Slice Bar
The first playable should prove all of these:
- the player can read witness truth without depending on a permanent HUD meter
- the player can intentionally succeed through a risky wrong route
- at least one failure result continues play in an altered state instead of forcing an immediate reset
- the space remembers what kind of arrival occurred
- one relationship or institution changes because of a mechanical route outcome

## Fun Rule
Originality is not enough.

If a mechanic sounds brilliant in design notes but is not fun within a short hands-on test, it does not deserve protection just because it is unusual.

## Design Rule
If the project can be converted into a visual novel without losing its strongest qualities, then the project is not game-first enough yet.
