# Relationship Principles

## Purpose
This project should not rely on clean friendship, predictable romance, or standard mentor-rival-family structures. Core bonds must feel intimate, dangerous, and spiritually expensive.

## Core Rule
Every major relationship should contain all four:

- devotion
- exploitation
- asymmetry
- a truth that would wound the bond if fully spoken

If a bond only provides comfort, exposition, or simple loyalty, it is too weak for this project.

## Required Ingredients

### 1. Mutual Need Must Be Morally Uneven
One person should need something from the other that they should not be allowed to need.

### 2. Tenderness Must Risk Desecration
Care should preserve someone while also endangering, deforming, or misnaming them.

### 3. The World Must Take Sides
The setting should not remain neutral. Laws of name, blood, vow, or inheritance should privilege one side of the bond and punish the other.

### 4. Revelation Must Make Earlier Affection Worse
A later truth should not erase tenderness. It should corrupt it, deepen it, or make it harder to forgive.

## Forbidden Easy Dynamics
- supportive childhood friend with no corrupting motive
- mentor who is simply wise until a late betrayal twist
- romance that heals both people without demanding a terrible cost
- rival who becomes an ally through generic mutual respect
- parent-child bond that is only protective and not also possessive, substitutive, or sacrificial
- villain whose obsession is only jealousy, revenge, or power hunger

## Preferred Twisted Bond Patterns

### 1. Shepherd And Vessel
One character protects another while also preparing them to become ritual material.

### 2. Replacement And Mourner
One character exists because another person was erased, tithed, or split apart. Love is inseparable from substitution.

### 3. Devotee And Counterfeit Saint
One character's worship gives another power, legitimacy, or survival, while also deforming who that person is allowed to become.

### 4. Executioner And Necessary Beloved
One character is sworn to kill, erase, or tithe the other if they become too real to safely keep.

### 5. Split Heirs
Two characters embody mutually exclusive claims to the same lineage, future, or sacred role. The world cannot recognize both at once.

## Design Tests
- Does each core bond contain at least one private ritual only those two characters share?
- Does each core bond have a concrete world consequence when strained or broken?
- Could both characters describe the bond as love while meaning radically different things?
- Would the bond still feel dangerous even if no overt villain acted on it?
- Is the bond attractive enough that someone would want to keep digging into it rather than only understand it once?

## Chapter Rule
At least one central relationship in every chapter should change:

- a location's meaning
- a rule of access
- a naming or inheritance claim
- a player-facing mechanic or choice

If the bond does not reshape the world, it is not yet strong enough.
