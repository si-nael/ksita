# Character Appeal

## Purpose
The world should naturally generate characters people want to choose favorites from, argue about, ship, fear, and obsess over.

This is not a cast list. It is a rulebook for making future characters feel diggable.

## The Character Hook Stack
Every important character should have all six.

### 1. Surface Hook
A one-line reason to care immediately.

Examples:
- the Bell Veil who can keep a bridge real but forgets their own face after each correction
- the Lantern Runner who is never allowed to travel unwitnessed even once
- the Turner who can make you socially unreal but still keeps one illegal person Held True

### 2. Signature Look
A silhouette, prop, or visual trait that reads before dialogue.

### 3. Public Role
A title the world knows them by.

### 4. Private Contradiction
The one desire, attachment, or secret witness relation that should not fit their role.

### 5. World-Law Connection
Their existence should teach something about witness, drift, Sequence, or Hollow travel.

### 6. Scene Promise
The player should be able to imagine at least one scene only that character could deliver.

## Character Dig Tests
- Could someone get interested in this character from their title and silhouette alone?
- Does their design embody one Hollow law rather than floating above the world?
- Do they have at least one bond that is attractive and dangerous at the same time?
- Would removing them make the world less emotionally specific?
- Can they generate fan-theory questions without becoming a pure mystery box?

## Role Families
These are world-facing role seeds, not fixed canon characters.

### Bell Veil
A route keeper whose face is hidden behind witness silk and bell halos. Publicly they preserve crossings. Privately they risk disappearing into whatever they hold true too often.

### Lantern Runner
A sanctioned courier who carries portable witness through blind wards. Fast, glamorous, and fragile: a person trusted to deliver reality while never fully belonging to themselves.

### Return Singer
A person whose voice can call others back into safe relation during the Returning Turn. They can become adored, depended on, and resented in equal measure.

### Last Looker
The one who seals sleepers, the dying, or the disappearing into Veiling. Intimate by role, lonely by design.

### Backcaller
An illegal counterpart to the Return Singer. Their voice can become someone's wrong home.

### Halo Thief
Someone glamorous enough to counterfeit public route truth and reckless enough to enjoy it.

### Afterkept
A person still being held after they should have become Unkept. Half miracle, half disaster.

### Twice-Called
A person being claimed by both lawful Return and private Backcall. Their body, shadow, and home-sense no longer agree on where they belong.

### Turner
A feared official who can perform Turning Away. Their emotional hook only works if at least one person exists whom they cannot bear to unmake.

### Face Twin
Two people recorded under one public face pattern. The world accepts them unevenly, creating instant asymmetry and obsession.

### Drift Bride
Someone kept singular only because they are still being awaited. If that waiting stops, their body and route presence begin to loosen.

### Blind Facer
A person who does not see through sight but through alignment of bells, breath, and relation. They can read correct witness more accurately than anyone else, which makes them both precious and isolated.

### False Saint
A hostile witness icon that looks trustworthy enough to Hold Wrong. As a character type, this can be tragic, seductive, or terrifying.

### Unfaced Foundling
A person who never received proper First Facing and therefore slips through route logic other people depend on. Dangerous, charismatic, and hard to place.

## Pair Appeal Rules
The Hollow should generate pairings and rivalries people want to think about even before the main plot exists.

Preferred pair energy:
- protector and illegal exception
- public handler and private witness
- sanctioned self and misrecognized self
- one person held true by the world and one held true by only one other person
- public caller and private backcaller
- the living and the afterkept

## Design Rule
If a character is only functional, symbolic, or tragic but not magnetic, they are not ready.
