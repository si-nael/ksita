# Yun First Wrong-Home Setpiece

## Purpose
Script the first major Returning conflict as gameplay rather than as a disguised cutscene.

## Setpiece Job
This sequence must prove all of these at once:
- Returning is the Hollow's strongest early conflict
- player action can hold or deform the scene
- music and gameplay peak together
- arrival state changes the emotional resolution
- the player chooses a side through spatial play, not only through dialogue

## Arena Layout

### 1. Main Span
The primary bridge where Lune enters while carrying the Relay Lantern.

### 2. Iri Platform
The lawful public call point.

What it communicates:
- elegance
- legitimacy
- visible strain under pressure

### 3. Nao Ledge
The blind-side private call point.

What it communicates:
- intimacy
- temptation
- wrongness that does not feel empty

### 4. Yun Centerline
The unstable midpoint where lawful and private witness collide.

### 5. Sera Entry Pier
Late-arrival enforcement vector.

## Entry States

### Lawful Entry
The player stayed on sanctioned routes.

Effects:
- cleaner opening bridge state
- stronger initial public trust
- less immediate suspicion from the Common Vigil

### Wrong-Route Entry
The player misarrived intentionally and recovered from the Nao-side ledge.

Effects:
- access to Frayed Bell Thread
- higher suspicion
- easier recognition of the private witness pattern

## Phase Structure

### Phase 1 | Loaded Approach
Duration target: 20 to 25 s

Player task:
- maintain the Relay Lantern across the Main Span
- read the lawful bridge state while hearing a private harmonic leak from the side

What appears:
- Iri visible ahead in clean witness alignment
- Nao not fully visible yet, but their backcall colors the route edge
- Yun present but not yet fully split

Failure result:
- if the player loses the route here, they enter the next phase in Split Recovery instead of hard resetting

### Phase 2 | Double Call Read
Duration target: 20 to 30 s

Player task:
- use Focus to distinguish the lawful bell phrase from the private backcall
- decide whether to keep a clean line or lean closer to the false source for tactical benefit

Key readability rule:
Held Wrong must look beautiful enough to tempt, but different enough to read.

### Phase 3 | Hold The Middle
Duration target: 30 to 45 s

Trigger:
Yun becomes visibly Twice-Called.

Player task:
- carry the Relay Lantern through three unstable anchor points
- use Quick-Step across one sight break
- optionally Knot one failing segment to keep the center viable

What this proves:
- Lune is not watching the scene happen; Lune is physically preventing collapse
- the bridge is a relationship problem made mechanical

### Phase 4 | Spatial Choice Window
Duration target: 10 to 15 s

Trigger:
Yun reaches the centerline and the bridge enters a controlled wrong-home crisis.

Player choices should remain in-world and spatial:

#### Support Iri
- move into the lawful witness band
- face Iri's platform
- press Call while the lawful phrase is strongest

#### Support Nao
- move into the false witness band
- face the blind-side ledge
- press Call while the backcall phrase is strongest

#### Hold The Middle
- stay centered
- maintain the lantern line
- Cut the strongest side claim at the last safe beat

This third option should be harder and less obviously advertised. It is the first mastery-style resolution.

## Resolution States

### A. Lawful Return
Likely from lawful entry or precise late correction.

Result:
- Yun's body arrives under public truth
- the shadow still lags in a way that proves the wound is not gone
- Iri's standing rises but emotional damage deepens
- Nao's trace remains in the district as future danger

Gameplay consequence:
- safer immediate revisit
- weaker access to private routes

### B. Wrong Home
Likely from wrong-route entry or deliberate backcall support.

Result:
- Yun's answer-name and shadow choose Nao before the body fully does
- the scene ends on beauty that is obviously catastrophic
- Sera's response becomes colder and more punitive

Gameplay consequence:
- private route access increases
- Common Vigil suspicion increases
- changed revisit becomes mechanically harsher in public space and richer in private space

### C. Broken Middle
Likely from unstable execution or risky neutrality.

Result:
- no side wins cleanly
- Yun leaves the scene more severely split
- everyone present has reason to mistrust everyone else more

Gameplay consequence:
- revisit routes become less stable overall
- rescue pressure increases in the next short segment
- the player keeps agency but pays for indecision or poor control

## Frayed Bell Thread Interaction
If the player obtained Frayed Bell Thread earlier:
- one failing cadence can be stabilized during Phase 3 or Phase 4
- this does not remove the moral cost of the route choice
- it only expands the player's margin for an advanced outcome

## Music Direction By Phase

### Phase 1
Clear public rhythm with one private harmonic stain beneath it.

### Phase 2
The wrong harmony becomes persuasive rather than merely threatening.

### Phase 3
Percussive route tension rises while melody remains unresolved.

### Phase 4
The score should peak while the player is still moving, aiming, and calling.

### Resolution
The music should not say which result is morally correct.
It should say which truth won and how much it cost.

## Fail-Forward Rule
The player should not be kicked to a generic retry screen unless they fully lose the route into unrecoverable drift.

Most mistakes should instead produce:
- Split Recovery
- worse revisit conditions
- altered suspicion
- a harsher version of the setpiece resolution

## Anti-Cutscene Rule
The scene may use short lock-ins for framing, but the decisive beats must remain playable:
- carrying the bridge
- reading the double call
- choosing which call to support
- living with the arrival state

## Prototype Script Requirement
When implemented, this setpiece should be built as an encounter controller with explicit phase transitions, not as a timeline of cinematics.
