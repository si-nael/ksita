# Emotional Targets

## Purpose
Translate the user's reference feelings into project-specific emotional goals without copying another work's plot, characters, setting skeleton, or presentation formula.

## Core Rule
Take effects, not structures.

Allowed:
- emotional cadence
- scale of catharsis
- severity of recontextualization
- the feeling of playable crescendo

Forbidden:
- borrowing character templates
- borrowing chapter skeletons
- borrowing faction structures
- borrowing cosmology ladders
- borrowing signature scenes and simply repainting them with Hollow terms

## Target 1 | Gameplay And Music Catharsis
The player should sometimes feel emotionally overwhelmed while they are still actively playing, not only after control is taken away.

### Desired Feeling
- rising momentum during live play
- music that makes a crossing feel larger than the mechanics alone
- beauty, sorrow, and determination hitting at once
- the sense that the player is carrying something impossible but still pushing through it

### Design Translation
- major route climaxes should keep the player in control during the emotional peak
- route states should affect music layering so Held True, Thinning, Held Wrong, and Lost do not only look different; they should feel musically different
- chapter climaxes should include at least one playable musical swell, not just a cutscene payoff after the hard part is over
- signature motifs should belong to roles, bonds, and route conditions, not only to places
- the first 20 minutes should already prove that crossing can feel exhilarating before the player understands the full story

### Music Direction Rule
- sanctioned witness should sound clear, formal, forward-driving, and spatially stable
- Keeping should sound intimate, wrong, seductive, and harmonically persuasive rather than merely sinister
- Held Wrong should sound beautiful in a way that feels slightly misresolved instead of simply dangerous
- Lost should not only become quiet; it should feel directionless and unheld

## Target 2 | Story And World Tragedy
The long-form story should hurt because personal bonds and world law are inseparable.

### Desired Feeling
- intimate pain inside mythic scale
- people carrying cosmic or civilizational pressure in their bodies and relationships
- repeated sacrifices that change meaning as the world becomes clearer
- tragedy that enlarges the world instead of shrinking it into shock value

### Design Translation
- every major world reveal should deepen at least one central bond rather than interrupting character drama
- the world should feel like it has already wounded everyone important before the player fully understands how
- acts of rescue, return, denial, and keeping should leave visible mechanical and emotional scars
- escalating scope should never detach from favorite-character pressure
- the world should become more heartbreaking as it becomes more legible

## Target 3 | Recontextualization Twist Bar
The best twists should feel less like a cheap betrayal and more like a terrifyingly elegant proof.

### Desired Feeling
- sudden clarity
- "of course that is what this meant"
- one idea collapsing many earlier contradictions into a sharper truth
- admiration and dread arriving together

### Design Translation
- major twists should come from a lawful reinterpretation of something already present in play
- a reveal should sharpen at least three earlier scenes rather than replacing them
- the strongest surprises should feel inevitable in retrospect, not merely hidden
- avoid twists whose only engine is withheld information, secret lineage, or a clean traitor reveal
- the ideal reveal is a single conceptual turn that makes earlier pain, beauty, and behavior all read differently at once

## Emotional Balance Rules
- beauty should arrive before brutality
- attachment should arrive before horror
- player agency should survive into catharsis instead of stopping right before it
- aftermath should matter as much as the peak itself
- tenderness should often make later scenes worse, not safer

## Hollow-Specific Benchmark
If the project reaches its intended bar, the player should sometimes feel all three of these at once:
- "this is gorgeous"
- "this is unbearable"
- "this makes too much sense now"

## Design Rule
If a planned emotional peak only works because it resembles another famous scene, rebuild it until the Hollow can own it completely.
