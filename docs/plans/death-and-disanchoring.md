# Death And Disanchoring

Status: baseline locked for worldbuilding. Death is now defined strongly enough to support future character and scene design.

## One-Sentence Rule
In the Hollow, death happens when a person can no longer be returned as one coherent self under any stable relation.

## The Important Distinction
Damage is not death.
Distance is not death.
Sleep is not death.
Drift is not always death.

Death begins when relation fails beyond correction.

## Body Continuity Rule
The body is an anchor, not the whole person.

A person can survive horrifying bodily harm if name, witness, route memory, and relational hold remain strong enough to rebind them.

A person can die with an almost untouched body if the world can no longer hold them as the same continuing self.

## The Seven States

### 1. Marked
Ordinary injury.

Meaning:
The body has been harmed, but the person's relation is still coherent.

Scene feel:
Blood, fractures, burns, and cracks can all happen here, but the person is still plainly themselves.

### 2. Thinning
Coherence loss has started.

Meaning:
The person is still present, but witness, silhouette, route sense, or self-answer is beginning to fray.

Scene feel:
This is the first truly frightening state because the person can often still speak, plead, or act normally.

### 3. Split
Part of the person fails to arrive, remain, or stay synchronized.

Meaning:
A voice, wound, memory habit, shadow, or bodily certainty is left behind or displaced.

Scene feel:
This is the Hollow's signature injury state. It is visually striking and emotionally specific.

### 4. Veiled Sleep
Controlled nightly loosening.

Meaning:
Sleep is safe only because Veiling confirms who gets to remain singular while unconscious.

Scene feel:
Tender, intimate, and unsettling. Someone usually must be the last one allowed to see you correctly.

### 5. Drift
The person is no longer being held by a stable enough relation, but may still be recoverable.

Meaning:
They become route-unsafe, identity-unstable, and increasingly likely to answer the wrong call.

Scene feel:
This is a rescue state, a fear state, and sometimes a temptation state.

### 6. Last Held
Only one relation, one witness, or one ritual chain still keeps the person coherently present.

Meaning:
The person remains reachable only because one specific someone, somewhere, or something refuses to let go.

Scene feel:
Extremely dramatic. This is where love, grief, duty, and obsession become physically identical.

### 7. Unkept
True death.

Meaning:
The person can no longer be returned as the same coherent self under any stable relation.

Scene feel:
Not always quiet. Sometimes beautiful. Always final in a way the Hollow makes frighteningly hard to admit.

## What Healing Means
Healing is not waiting.

Healing means re-binding:
- restoring correct witness
- correcting false route relation
- rejoining split elements
- stabilizing answer-name and bodily pattern
- relieving hostile weight or claim

This can happen quickly if the right relations are present, or fail completely if they are not.

## What Remains After Death
Unkept does not mean nothing remains.

Possible remains:
- body shell: the physical form or part of it
- answer residue: the way a person used to answer a name, touch, or bell call
- route habit: places still expecting them for a while
- shadow remnant: leftover movement, posture, or silhouette logic

These remains are dangerous because they can be loved, mistaken, exploited, or illegally kept.

## Public Death Versus Private Death

### Public Death
The Common Vigil confirms that no stable civic return remains possible.

### Private Death
One or more people refuse that conclusion and continue keeping the person through memory, shadow, knot, or backcall.

The Hollow allows this often enough to make grief far more dangerous than simple mourning.

## Why Death Is Emotionally Different Here
- someone can be dying because the wrong person stopped witnessing them
- a breakup, exile, or Turning Away can become physically lethal
- sleep scenes matter because Veiling is the difference between rest and loosening
- someone can remain present after death in a way that is sincere, loving, and catastrophically wrong

## Role Pressure This Creates
- Last Lookers become intimate by profession
- Return Singers may be asked to call someone back one final time and fail
- Afterkeepers become unbearable and irresistible figures
- anyone declared Unkept can still become the center of forbidden attachment

## Common Vigil Position
The Common Vigil prefers clean Veiling, confirmed death, and stable release.

It fears Afterkeep because prolonged private holding confuses law, route truth, inheritance, and household reality.

## Design Rule
If death in the Hollow can be reduced to "HP reached zero," this world has failed its own premise.
