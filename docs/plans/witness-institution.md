# Witness Institution

Status: baseline locked for worldbuilding. This institution is part of the Hollow's core structure, not an optional faction.

## Institution Name
The Common Vigil

## Public Names
- The Vigil
- Bell Veils
- Halo Keepers

## Vigil Term For Illegal Witness
Counterwitness.

The word is used for all unsanctioned private holding that attempts to decide reality outside public recognition.

## One-Sentence Purpose
The Common Vigil is the halo-veiled order that keeps people and routes Held True so they stay reachable, singular, and publicly real.

## Why It Matters
Private memory and personal affection are not enough to hold a whole region together in the Hollow.

Two lovers may keep each other near for a while. A family may hold one another coherent inside a home. But roads, ports, courts, stairways, bridges, markets, and the dead cannot rely on private witness alone. Large-scale civilization requires shared, repeatable, enforceable regard.

The Common Vigil exists because reality in the Hollow must be maintained as public agreement.

## Why People Are Drawn To It
- its members wear face veils, bell halos, and lantern rigs that make them look beautiful and dangerous
- it offers one of the strongest public identities in the Hollow: to be seen by the Vigil is to matter
- its rites decide who may cross, who may return, and who becomes real again after being nearly lost
- even people who fear it can become fascinated with the people inside it

## Core Principle
To be real in a civic sense is to be commonly witnessed.

Without Common Vigil recognition, a person may still exist emotionally, privately, or locally, but they become unstable in law, travel, access, and public memory.

## Core Functions

### 1. Civic Witness
The Common Vigil maintains public recognition of people, places, and routes so they can be used safely.

### 2. Route Maintenance
It staffs bell-eyes, witness lanterns, gaze-towers, and crossing frames that keep major routes Held True.

### 3. Personhood Registration
It records who is publicly held under what face, relation, kin-line, vow, or settlement claim.

### 4. Dispute Resolution
Many legal conflicts are settled by deciding whose witness has physical authority over a person, route, dwelling, corpse, or relic.

### 5. Sanction And Exile
It can reduce, suspend, or withdraw public witness. This is one of the most feared civic punishments in the Hollow.

## Public Infrastructure

### Bell-Eyes
Paired bells housed in opposing towers or frames. When properly maintained, they keep routes and civic thresholds aligned under agreed witness.

### Witness Lanterns
Portable or mounted witness sources used to carry sanctioned regard through blind zones, funerary passages, harbor crossings, and emergency repairs.

These should read as signature gear, not generic tools.

### Face Ledgers
Not books of past events. They are active civic records of who is currently held true, by whom, and under which relation.

### Regard Frames
Threshold structures placed at bridges, courts, homes, docks, and shrines. Passing through one does not merely prove identity; it helps stabilize it.

### Vigil Piers
Observation landings suspended over void routes. Witness crews work from these platforms in paired shifts so crossings do not loosen.

## Civic Rites

### First Facing
The rite by which a child, foundling, or newly recovered person is brought under stable public witness. Until First Facing is complete, travel and legal standing remain dangerous.

### The Return Roll
A repeated public witness rite performed in settlements so residents remain mutually held in common reality.

In practice, this is the Common Vigil's most public form of the Returning Turn.

### Crossing Grant
A sanctioned extension of witness that allows a traveler or cargo to move through sensitive routes that private regard cannot safely hold.

### Turning Away
Formal withdrawal of common witness from a person, household, or site. It is not always immediate annihilation, but it makes public reality stop working correctly around the target.

This rite should feel feared, intimate, and dramatic enough to anchor major character scenes.

## Visual Language
- pale face veils that blur at the edges when witness weakens
- suspended bell halos that align above the head during correct route holding
- black-gold lantern frames worn like reliquary harnesses
- witness silk, route hooks, and ledger seals carried as both tools and status symbols

## Character Appeal Pressure
The Common Vigil should not only function well as a system. It should also generate irresistible role types:

- the Bell Veil who holds everyone together except themselves
- the Lantern Runner who can cross blind wards but cannot belong anywhere privately
- the Turner everyone fears and someone still loves
- the Facer who can make another person publicly real but cannot keep their own secret bond legal

## How The Common Vigil Is Beautiful
- entire settlements remain coherent because hundreds of small acts of regard are kept in balance
- bells, lanterns, towers, and ledgers make civilization feel like a living act of collective attention
- the institution gives abandoned people a way to become publicly real again through First Facing
- its workers are literally holding the world together in visible, daily ways

## How The Common Vigil Is Cruel
- to protect the many, it standardizes how people may be publicly seen
- privacy is never fully innocent because unsanctioned witness can destabilize routes and law
- unofficial love, private mourning, secret names, and forbidden kinship can become civic threats
- Turning Away functions as a form of civil unmaking

## Social Consequences

### Legibility Is Safety
People with clean, current, well-supported Vigil standing move more safely through the world.

### Stigma Has Physical Force
If a household loses standing, routes may literally become harder for them to cross.

### Love Can Be Jurisdiction
To witness someone too strongly, or under the wrong relation, can create illegal proximity and false routes.

### Orphans, Exiles, And The Misrecognized Are At Risk
Anyone not securely held by kin, settlement, or institution is vulnerable to drift and hostile witness.

## Internal Structure

### Bell Keepers
Maintain paired route witness and route harmony.

### Facers
Perform direct civic recognition rites, including First Facing, dispute confirmations, and emergency re-anchoring.

### Ledger Hands
Maintain active face ledgers and relation records. Their work is legal, ritual, and physical at once.

### Turners
Officials empowered to enact suspension, correction, or withdrawal of public witness. Feared even within the institution.

## Prototype Role In The Vigil Span
The Common Vigil is the active institution governing the Vigil Span.

This means:
- paired bell-eyes are Common Vigil infrastructure
- witness lanterns used in the prototype are sanctioned tools, not generic puzzle props
- blind wards exist where Vigil coverage has failed, been sabotaged, or been withdrawn
- false witness events can be caused by damaged Vigil systems, corrupted Facers, or rival witness sources
- public Facing and Carrying in the area should visibly belong to the Vigil, while private Knotting and Veiling can leak outside its control

## Built-In Conflict Pressure
The institution is necessary, but it is intolerable in what it demands.

If it weakens, roads fail and settlements loosen. If it grows stronger, people become safer by becoming more publicly legible and less privately free.

This tension should remain active across the whole project.

## Design Rule
If the Common Vigil ever feels like a flavor-only religion or a generic state bureaucracy, it has failed. It must always feel like public attention turned into infrastructure.
