# World Foundation

Status: locked world direction. Story, protagonist, and chapter structure remain intentionally unlocked.

## World Name
The Hollow

## One-Sentence Law
In the Hollow, nothing changes because time passes; distance, heat, growth, decay, motion, and death change only when relations of witness, weight, naming, and position are rewritten.

## Core Premise
The world is not a planet, continent, or sky-bound realm. It is a void.

Habitations, roads, seas, shrines, and fields exist as anchored formations suspended over black depth. There is no natural horizon, no reliable ground, and no universal above or below. Stable places are held together by relational law rather than by time, geology, or ordinary celestial order.

People survive in the Hollow by maintaining Sequence: shared rituals, bells, chants, marks, and witness practices that impose a usable sense of before and after on a reality that does not physically require chronology. Sequence does not make time real. It only keeps minds, bodies, and settlements coherent enough to live.

Sequence is defined more concretely in `sequence-rites.md` as a chain of Five Turns: Facing, Carrying, Knotting, Returning, and Veiling.

If Sequence weakens, the world does not simply become chaotic later. It loosens immediately. Rooms hold multiple states at once. Wounds remain both closed and open until one claim wins authority. Journeys cease to be measured by duration and become dependent on relation, memory, and weight.

## What This World Must Feel Like
- non-terrestrial at first glance
- beautiful because it should not be possible and yet remains coherent
- dangerous because private emotion and public law can physically rearrange matter
- impossible to mistake for a normal fantasy kingdom, town, or wilderness

## Foundational Laws

### 1. Time Is A Mental Convenience, Not A Physical Driver
People experience sequence, anticipation, and recollection, but matter does not obey elapsed time. Clocks, bells, calendars, and rituals are social stabilizers rather than cosmic truths.

### 2. Change Is Relational
Nothing moves, heals, burns, rots, cools, or grows because seconds pass. Change occurs when the relations around a thing are rewritten. Witness, vow, distance, naming, grief, hunger, and claim all have physical force.

### 3. Distance Is Not Measured Only By Length
Two locations can become near because they are mutually remembered, ritually aligned, or claimed by the same law. A nearby ledge can become unreachable if its relation to the traveler has been severed.

### 4. Witness Anchors Reality
Things remain singular when enough stable witness holds them together. Unwitnessed objects, places, and people drift toward plurality, contradiction, or dispersal.

### 5. Death Is Disanchoring
Death is not a moment on a timeline. A being dies when its relations can no longer hold it as one continuing form. Corpses, echoes, names, and gestures may remain active if enough anchoring force still claims them.

The full ladder from injury to Unkept is defined in `death-and-disanchoring.md`.

### 6. The Void Eats The Unclaimed
Anything not held by relation eventually loosens into the Hollow. This is not weather or darkness. It is ontological starvation.

## Physics Without Time

### Motion
Movement is a change of relation, not position-over-time. Paths open when the traveler's weight, name, or witness matches the path's law.

Baseline travel rules are defined in `travel-and-distance.md`.

### Fire
Flame persists while hunger is fed. A lamp can burn forever if it is still awaited. A bonfire can die instantly if its claim is broken.

### Growth
Living things grow by completing patterns, accepting names, or receiving sufficient witness. Age is cultural, not purely physical.

### Decay
Decay begins when continuity thins. A palace can remain immaculate for centuries of human counting and collapse at once when forgotten by the right witness.

### Gravity
Pull is situational. Mass matters, but so do devotion, mourning, ownership, and place-claim. Heavy sorrow can bend routes as surely as stone.

## Representative Place

### The Sea Beneath the Last Stair
An upside-down ocean hangs in the void under a descending cathedral stair that has no first step and no final landing. Pilgrim vessels do not sail on top of the sea but along the underside of its black surface, lit by lanterns that burn only while someone is still awaited.

Bells dropped from the stair do not fall through time. They arrive wherever grief currently has the strongest claim on them. Islands drift below the sea like drowned reliquaries, and the tide changes not by lunar cycle but by which names are being spoken nearby.

This place is the standard for the whole project. If a location could plausibly exist on Earth with a little fantasy decoration, it is not enough.

## Why The World Is Beautiful
- seas can hang under stairways and still obey law
- bridges can form from mutual memory rather than stonework alone
- settlements feel like collective acts of impossible maintenance
- ritual, emotion, and architecture visibly shape reality

## Why The World Is Cruel
- stable identity depends on other people holding you together correctly
- forgetting, misnaming, abandonment, or divided love can become physical harm
- societies must regulate witness and relation to remain livable
- intimacy is powerful enough to deform shared space

## Human Consequences
- people keep sequence through the Five Turns, using bells, answer-names, witness relays, knot marks, and closing regard
- legal disputes are often disputes over anchoring authority rather than historical fact
- burial, marriage, adoption, and exile all alter physics, not just social status
- solitude is dangerous because singular private truth cannot easily hold a person together for long
- access to places is political because witness, claim, and naming directly control mobility
- the Common Vigil turns public attention into civic infrastructure and can make someone safer or less real by changing how they are commonly witnessed
- illegal Keeping lets private attachment override public truth at terrible cost
- death is feared not only because people vanish, but because the wrong person may keep them after they should be gone

## World Rule For Future Design
Every major system, place, creature, and ritual must visibly prove at least one of these:

- the world is void, not terrestrial
- physics is independent of time
- relation has physical authority
- stable life requires collective anchoring
