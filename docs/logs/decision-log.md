# Decision Log

## DEC-001 | 2026-03-30 | Emotional Recontextualization First
The project will prioritize emotional recontextualization over content volume.

Reason:
The target feeling is not just surprise. It is the combination of attachment, dread, and reinterpretation. That only happens if the game reuses people, spaces, and lines in a way that changes their meaning.

## DEC-002 | 2026-03-30 | Original Identity Over Direct Emulation
The project will take inspiration from emotionally potent narrative games, but its signature should come from the memory-rewrite structure, not direct imitation of another game's combat or premise.

Reason:
The intended impact depends on distinctiveness. If the structure feels too familiar, even strong scenes will lose force.

## DEC-003 | 2026-03-30 | Planning And Logs Are Separate
Planning documents and work logs will stay in separate folders and files.

Reason:
Mixing long-term design direction with session-level notes causes drift and makes future updates harder to maintain.

## DEC-004 | 2026-03-30 | World Law Over Familiar Framing
The project will prioritize a distinctive civic-fantasy world with its own physical laws over more familiar school-life or symbolic-trial framing.

Reason:
The user explicitly values a unique, attractive world and conflict between people and the structure they live inside. The setting itself must do heavy dramatic work.

## DEC-005 | 2026-03-30 | The World Is A Primary Antagonist
The story will center on characters trying to remain whole inside a system that rewards self-editing.

Reason:
This creates stronger long-form tension than a purely interpersonal mystery and gives the project a more distinctive identity.

## DEC-006 | 2026-03-30 | Explicit Anti-Derivation Guardrails
The project will maintain written boundaries against copying familiar structures from known emotional indie RPGs or magical-girl nightmare frameworks.

Reason:
Distinctiveness should be protected deliberately, not assumed.

## DEC-007 | 2026-03-30 | Mythic Fantasy Over Modern Frame
The project will favor mythic, ceremonial fantasy over modern or near-modern framing.

Reason:
The user explicitly prefers fantasy, and the current concept becomes more singular when its world law governs blood, vows, shrines, and land instead of modern civic systems.

## DEC-008 | 2026-03-30 | Twisted Bonds As A Design Requirement
Core relationships must be structurally strange, asymmetrical, and dangerous rather than familiar emotional archetypes.

Reason:
The user does not want obvious interpersonal dynamics. Relationship architecture must carry as much originality as the world itself.

## DEC-009 | 2026-03-30 | Quality Bar Must Be Written Down
The project will maintain an explicit quality bar document instead of relying on taste alone.

Reason:
Ambition without criteria leads to generic compromise. The design needs a kill rule for merely good but familiar ideas.

## DEC-010 | 2026-03-30 | World Selection Before Story Lock
The project will choose from multiple world candidates before locking story, protagonist details, or vertical-slice narrative.

Reason:
The user does not like the current story direction. World law must be chosen first so later story work grows from the correct foundation instead of forcing plot onto a weak setting.

## DEC-011 | 2026-03-30 | The World Is The Hollow
The selected world is the Hollow: a void rather than an Earth-like land world.

Reason:
The user explicitly wanted the world to be 공허, with representative places that do not exist on Earth.

## DEC-012 | 2026-03-30 | Physics Must Be Independent Of Time
The Hollow's physical laws will not depend on elapsed time.

Reason:
This gives the project a hard structural identity instead of a decorative one and forces every later design layer to become more original.

## DEC-013 | 2026-03-30 | Story Is Deferred Until Hollow Physics Are Concrete
Story and slice narrative should remain paused until the consequences of achronal physics are defined concretely.

Reason:
The user asked to build the setting one step at a time before returning to story.

## DEC-014 | 2026-03-30 | Travel Is Relational Qualification
Travel in the Hollow will work by qualifying for routes through witness, claim, weight, naming, and orientation rather than by crossing metric distance over elapsed time.

Reason:
This makes traversal mechanically inseparable from the world's physics instead of leaving the achronal law as background lore.

## DEC-015 | 2026-03-30 | First Prototype Uses Witness Authority
The first prototype area will be built around Witness as the dominant route authority.

Reason:
Witness is the clearest and most emotionally central way to prove that relation has physical force in the Hollow. It is readable, prototype-friendly, and thematically loaded.

## DEC-016 | 2026-03-30 | Witness Feedback Is Diegetic-First
Witness travel will be communicated primarily through the world, the body, and sound, with only a subtle systemic fallback.

Reason:
The system must be readable without collapsing into a generic HUD bar. The player should feel the world recognizing or misrecognizing them directly.

## DEC-017 | 2026-03-30 | Public Witness Is Governed By The Common Vigil
The Hollow's core witness-regulating institution is the Common Vigil.

Reason:
The world needs a necessary but intolerable institution that turns public attention into infrastructure, law, and punishment. This makes witness social, political, and physical at once.

## DEC-018 | 2026-03-30 | Subculture-Facing Tone Over Classical Tone
The project's presentation should lean toward stylish, character-hook-first fantasy rather than classical, archaic, or essay-like fantasy tone.

Reason:
The user explicitly prefers light-novel and anime-style appeal: a world that feels attractive, sharp, and full of characters worth digging into.

## DEC-019 | 2026-03-30 | Sequence Is A Five-Turn Ritual Relay
Sequence will be built from the Five Turns of Facing, Carrying, Knotting, Returning, and Veiling rather than treated as abstract civic timekeeping.

Reason:
The user wanted the world to feel more character-driven and less like social commentary. Turning Sequence into role-based ritual relay creates stronger scene types, character hooks, and private conflict.

## DEC-020 | 2026-03-30 | Illegal Witness Is Keeping
Unsanctioned private witness in the Hollow will be called Counterwitness publicly and Keeping in common emotional language.

Reason:
The setting needed a more intimate and character-magnetic counterforce to the Common Vigil. Keeping makes forbidden attachment physically meaningful.

## DEC-021 | 2026-03-30 | Witness Uses A Dual Model
Witness will function as both a local traversal state and a broader narrative standing.

Reason:
This preserves immediate readability in play while letting social recognition and route access matter over longer arcs without reducing witness to a generic meter.

## DEC-022 | 2026-03-30 | Returning Anchors Early Character Conflict
The first major character-focused conflict should center on the Returning Turn.

Reason:
"Who gets to call you home" is the Hollow's strongest built-in emotional question and naturally pits public order against private attachment.

## DEC-023 | 2026-03-30 | Death Culminates In Becoming Unkept
True death in the Hollow will be defined as becoming Unkept: no stable relation can return the person as the same coherent self.

Reason:
This keeps death inseparable from the world's relational law and makes grief, sleep, rescue, and illegal keeping dramatically distinct.

## DEC-024 | 2026-03-30 | First Role Cluster Is The Returning Cluster
The first character design pass should build from a Returning conflict cluster rather than from isolated cool roles.

Reason:
The world now has enough structure that it should immediately produce character magnetism. Returning and Keeping create the strongest built-in emotional and visual collision.

## DEC-025 | 2026-03-30 | First Named Seed Set Uses Five Characters
The first character seed pass will use five named figures: Iri, Nao, Yun, Lune, and Sera.

Reason:
The world needs concrete people to test whether the Hollow produces obsession-worthy character dynamics rather than only attractive roles.

## DEC-026 | 2026-03-30 | Render Direction Is 2D-First And Non-Photoreal
The Hollow should use a 2D-first, non-photoreal visual direction. Pixel or pastel are both acceptable. Realistic or generic 3D presentation should be avoided unless it becomes uniquely unmistakable.

Reason:
The user explicitly allowed dot or pastel approaches and asked to avoid realistic or dimensional presentation unless it could be made truly distinctive.

## DEC-027 | 2026-03-30 | Lune Is The First POV Anchor
Lune will anchor the first sustained perspective.

Reason:
Lune can carry traversal, witness logic, and relationship tension without turning the opening into static exposition or a one-sided faction view.

## DEC-028 | 2026-03-30 | First Arc Tone Is Seductive-Tragic
The first arc should feel seductive and emotionally magnetic before it turns openly predatory.

Reason:
The project works best if attachment forms before horror resolves into clear danger.

## DEC-029 | 2026-03-30 | 80-Hour Target Means Completionist Scope, Not Main-Route Bloat
The project may target roughly 60 to 80 hours for full completion, but the first committed clear should remain closer to 20 to 30 hours.

Reason:
The Hollow depends on density, not repetition. If the main route is stretched to 80 hours, the strongest world and relationship material will likely dilute. The longer target should come from optional routes, mastery, replay divergence, and achievement-linked content.

## DEC-030 | 2026-03-30 | The Hollow Must Prove Itself As A Real Game
The project must not drift into a visual novel, walking simulator, or story-first structure with decorative interaction.

Reason:
The user explicitly wants something that feels like a real game and values innovation. The Hollow's world law only earns its place if recognition, traversal, failure, and social consequence are active systems rather than lore wrapped around dialogue.

## DEC-031 | 2026-03-30 | Fun Is A System Requirement, Not A Bonus
The project will treat immediate hands-on fun as a non-negotiable filter for every major mechanic.

Reason:
Originality and atmosphere are not enough. If a route system is only interesting after explanation and not enjoyable in motion, it will not sustain a real game no matter how good the worldbuilding is.

## DEC-032 | 2026-03-30 | Reference Feelings Must Be Translated, Not Copied
The project may use the user's favorite emotional highs as benchmarks, but only by translating them into Hollow-specific targets for gameplay, music, tragedy, and reveal design.

Reason:
The user wants extremely high emotional payoff. That is useful as a quality target, but the project must protect its own identity. The correct move is to extract what the feelings are doing, then rebuild them through Hollow systems instead of borrowing another work's structure.

## DEC-033 | 2026-03-30 | First Slice Uses Real-Time Focused Crossing
The first playable will use fully real-time movement with a short focus-hold state, a 2D oblique action view, and the Relay Lantern as its first portable witness mechanic.

Reason:
This format preserves urgency, lets music and play peak together, stays prototype-friendly in the current production direction, and is readable enough to teach witness law without turning the project into either a visual novel or a turn-based tactics game.

## DEC-034 | 2026-03-30 | Early Climax Choices Must Be Spatial, Not Menu-Driven
The first wrong-home climax will be decided through movement, positioning, route maintenance, and a contextual Call action rather than through a detached dialogue choice menu.

Reason:
The project's emotional peaks need to remain playable. If Returning collapses into a dialogue picker at the decisive beat, the game will lose the very identity it is trying to prove.
