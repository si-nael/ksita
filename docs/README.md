## Operating Rules
1. Update the changelog whenever deliverables, scope, or direction changes.
2. Update the decision log whenever a choice is locked, rejected, or reversed.
3. Append one work-log entry per working session, even if the session only covered planning.
4. Keep `master-plan.md` stable. New speculative ideas should go to `open-questions.md` until they are tested.
5. If a topic becomes large enough to deserve its own document, add it under `docs/plans` and link it here.
6. Protect distinctiveness explicitly. If a new idea feels too close to an obvious reference work, record the concern in `creative-boundaries.md` or `decision-log.md` and redirect it.
7. If a relationship or plot beat feels emotionally safe or easy to predict, rework it before it becomes canon.
8. Do not lock story, characters, or slice structure until the world law is stable enough to carry them.
9. Do not let worldbuilding become drier than the characters it is supposed to support.
10. Do not let story prestige replace playable identity; if a feature reads better than it plays, rework it.

## Log Format
- Use `YYYY-MM-DD` in section headers.
- Keep entries short, specific, and action-oriented.
- Prefer decisions that can be tested quickly in a prototype.
